## Full Description

Psilocybin Research Tracker is a research-focused literature app for exploring psilocybin and psilocin publications, preprints, clinical trial records, citation relationships, journals, authors, topics, and source coverage.

The app is built for researchers, clinicians, journalists, policy analysts, and advanced readers who need source-labeled bibliographic context rather than promotional or medical advice.

Key features:

- Search psilocybin and psilocin publications by keyword, author, journal, source, publication status, topic, study type, substance, and date.
- Browse latest indexed records from PubMed, Crossref, Europe PMC, OpenAlex, preprint servers, and ClinicalTrials.gov.
- Distinguish published records, preprints, protocols, reviews, and clinical trial registry records.
- Explore a citation network for connected papers, authors, topics, journals, and cited references.
- Review publication trends and analytics.
- Export records from the web app in BibTeX, RIS, CSV, JSON, LaTeX, and plain citation formats.
- Subscribe to optional email or push alerts for newly indexed psilocybin and psilocin records.
- Use an installed app shell with a branded offline fallback page.

Important:

This app is a bibliographic research tool. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, dosing guidance, or instructions for substance use. Always verify records in the original source before relying on them for research, clinical, legal, or policy work.
